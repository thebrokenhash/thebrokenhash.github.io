Humans of Game

We just adored the way Humans of *insert any place here* tell stories of the fellows we have never met and probably will never meet but still somehow feel connected. These stories impact us enough to carve some space in our hearts.

What we thought was that they should be remembered, not actually remembered but somewhere in our brains consciously or unconsciously they should live, reinforced, and keep us intrigued.

Thus to make this happen we started working on creating a game which tests us on the stories we read, how many people we know and how well are we connected to the page.

Up, Up and Above

The Bhils are the second largest tribal community in western and central India. For generations they have passed their knowledge to the new generations through the simple activity of drawing, sculpting and painting.

They believe painting to be the best form of prayer and healing. Everything that is connected to the daily life of the Bhil is painted; this includes the animals, trees, mythological figures, and their daily activities using dots.

There are many stories that are connected to the use of dots. Most believe that each dot of the painting is a representation of their ancestor. This game is based on two important traditions and beliefs.

Firstly, It is a kind of game in which young Bhil boys and girls participate. The head of the village (Mukhiya) is responsible to arrange this game at the cross road of the main market. They fix a huge 40 feet wooden log on the ground that is already made very slippery by applying soap and oil on its surface. On the top of the log they hang coconuts that have been set as a target for the boys. When the group of boys starts climbing on it the girls try hit them with a stick to stop them. But whoever succeeds to reach the top and grab the coconut wins the game and can ask any girl to marry whoever they want to.

Secondly, Bhil community believes in conserving water. Thus water is used as a currency to make advancements in the game.
